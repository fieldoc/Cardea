Bottom Half Explorations

PULSE hero (top) is locked. Four layouts for stats + progress + coaching below — all fit without scrolling.

Centered ring + peripherals

Fused StripEverything compressed into three horizontal bands: stats as bare numbers, progress as a single bar with mini ring, coaching as a tight card. Maximum data density, zero scroll.
Volume BarsDistance and time shown as inline progress bars between stats and coaching. Each bar has a subtle gradient fill matching the Cardea spectrum.
Custom Icon: Pulse EchoThe coaching icon is a miniature ECG peak — echoing the hero waveform above. Ties the whole screen together as one biometric narrative.
Data TilesA 2x2 grid gives each metric its own breathing room: Goal progress, Streak count, Bootcamp week, and Volume. Coaching insight spans full width at the bottom.
Tile IconsEach tile has a custom Cardea icon in the header — gradient-stroked SVGs that feel native to the design system. No emoji anywhere.
Custom Icon: Ascending FlameThe coaching icon is an upward flame — representing momentum and energy. Drawn with the Cardea gradient stroke, not a generic emoji.
Ambient BandsFull-width horizontal layers separated by subtle gradient hairlines. No cards, no borders — just floating data in clean rows. The most minimal approach.
Breathing RoomSpace is distributed evenly between bands using flexbox justify-content: space-evenly. The content breathes without feeling sparse.
Custom Icon: Compass RoseA tiny directional compass — representing guidance and direction. The Cardea gradient rotates through its 4-stop spectrum around the compass points.
Ring FocusThe progress ring is the centerpiece of the bottom half — larger than in the original but smaller than ORBIT. Stats float at the cardinal positions (left, right, bottom).
Volume + Coaching CollapsedTwo thin strips at the bottom: volume as inline bars, coaching as a one-liner. Everything above the nav without scrolling.
Custom Icon: Heart Rate PeakA stylized HR spike — a single QRS complex from an ECG. The most Cardea-native icon possible, drawn with the 4-stop gradient.
