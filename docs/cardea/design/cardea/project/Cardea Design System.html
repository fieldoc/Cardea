Principles

Every screen should feel calm, precise, premium. Never colorful, busy, or gamified. Accent color must remain rare and meaningful — used only for active recording, the primary CTA, and the brand mark.

01 / PRINCIPLE

Glass over chrome

Information lives inside soft, subtly-lit glass cards — never raw on the background. Borders, not fills, do the structural work.

flat fills→glass + 1px border
02 / PRINCIPLE

Gradient is rare

The brand gradient signals identity, primary action, and live data. Reserve it. Re-coloring everything kills the signal.

recolor all→CTA · graphs · ring
03 / PRINCIPLE

Data first, motion second

Numbers and lines lead. Animations are 250ms ease-out — fades and positional shifts only. No bounce, no decoration.

spring · bounce→250ms ease-out

Surfaces

Three glass tiers. Default for everything; focused for nav and elevated modals; emphasis for the single most important card on a screen — typically Today's Session.

Tier 1 · Default

Glass card

Every metric, every list, every chart. White α 0.05 → 0.015 fill, 1px α 0.08 border, 16–18px radius.

fill rgba(255,255,255,0.05→0.015)
border rgba(255,255,255,0.08)
radius 16–18px
blur 12–20px
Tier 2 · Focused

Elevated glass

Bottom navigation, modals, sheets. Brighter fill and a slightly more visible border to lift it off the canvas.

fill rgba(255,255,255,0.08→0.03)
border rgba(255,255,255,0.12)
radius 12–16px
blur 16px
Tier 3 · Emphasis

Brand-ringed

Reserved for the screen's primary card — Today's Session, post-run summary hero. 1px gradient ring, faint gradient wash inside.
