Heart Rate Sensing
Bluetooth chest strap reads your heartbeat once per second
▼
BLE connection — pairs with a Bluetooth Low Energy heart rate monitor (e.g. Coospo H808S chest strap). Reconnects automatically if signal drops.
HR stream — delivers a real-time stream of heart rate values (beats per minute) that feeds every downstream system.

GPS Tracking
Phone GPS tracks distance, pace, and route
▼
Distance accumulation — tracks total distance run in meters, used to determine which workout segment you're in.
Pace calculation — computes smoothed pace (min/km) from GPS speed, using a weighted moving average (72% previous, 28% instant) to filter GPS jitter.

Data Quality Filters
Three guards that catch bad sensor data before it reaches the coaching brain
▼
Cadence Lock Detector — optical HR sensors sometimes "lock" onto your arm swing rhythm instead of your heartbeat. Detects this by looking for a sudden HR jump (25+ bpm) followed by an unnaturally flat plateau (±3 bpm for 8+ seconds). When detected: suppresses HRmax auto-calibration and flags TRIMP as unreliable.
Auto-Pause Detector — uses a Schmitt trigger on GPS speed to detect when you've stopped (e.g. at a traffic light). Stops at <1.8 km/h for 3 seconds, resumes at >3.6 km/h. Pauses distance/time tracking so your metrics aren't diluted by standing still.
Environment Flag — after the run, checks if heat, humidity, or altitude skewed your HR data. If aerobic decoupling is abnormally high AND pace is slower than your baseline at the same HR, the session is flagged so the learning engine ignores it when assessing your fitness trend.

🎯
Zone Evaluation
"Am I above, below, or in my target heart rate zone right now?"
▼
Target zone — your current target HR comes from the workout config. In steady-state mode it's one fixed number; in interval/distance mode it changes per segment. The zone is target ± a buffer (typically 3-5 bpm).
Hysteresis — once you're IN the zone, you get 2 extra bpm of leeway before being declared OUT. This prevents rapid zone-flipping when your HR hovers near the boundary.
Result → IN_ZONE, ABOVE_ZONE, BELOW_ZONE, or NO_DATA
🧠
Adaptive Prediction Engine
"Where will my HR be in 10-15 seconds?" — the core learning system
▼
HR Slope Tracking — measures whether your heart rate is currently rising or falling, and how fast. Smoothed exponentially (75/25 blend). A slope of +2 bpm/min means "HR is climbing steadily."
Pace-HR Lookup — remembers what heart rate your body typically produces at each pace. Groups paces into buckets (e.g. "6:00-6:15 min/km → avg 148 bpm"). If your current pace usually makes your HR run high, the prediction accounts for that.
Short-Term Trim — a within-workout correction that tracks how wrong recent predictions have been and nudges future predictions to compensate. Decays at 86% per tick — it reacts quickly but forgets quickly. Captures things like "I'm warming up, HR is running higher than model expects."
Long-Term Trim — a cross-session correction that persists between runs. If your HR consistently runs 3 bpm higher than targets, the trim learns this bias and bakes it into all future predictions. Decays at 92% per session — very slow, very stable. Capped at ±20 bpm.
Response Lag — how many seconds your HR takes to respond after you change pace. Measured by timing how long it takes to return to zone after drifting out. Starts at 25s, refined each session. Typical range: 8-90s. Controls the prediction horizon (how far ahead we look).
Projection Confidence — how much to trust the prediction. LOW (first 1-2 sessions): no projection shown. MEDIUM (2-4 sessions): hedged language like "may drift up." HIGH (4+ sessions): confident language like "drifting up."
The Prediction Formula — projected HR = current HR + (slope × horizon) + (short trim × 0.35) + long trim + pace bias

Coaching Language System
Decides what to say, when to say it, and how urgently — three layers working together
▼
Layer 1: Guidance Text Generation
Context-sensitive language — the text you see on screen is generated by combining your actual zone, projected zone, and HR trend. Examples:
Above zone, HR falling:"Above zone — HR falling" Above zone, HR not falling:"Above zone — ease off now" Below zone, HR rising:"Below zone — HR rising" Below zone, HR not rising:"Below zone — pick up pace now" In zone, projected above (high):"HR climbing — ease off slightly" In zone, projected above (medium):"HR trending up — ease off slightly" In zone, projected below (high):"HR dropping — pick up slightly" In zone, projected below (medium):"HR trending low — pick up slightly" In zone, low confidence, HR moving:"In zone — HR rising" / "HR falling" In zone, low confidence, calm:"Learning your patterns — hold steady" In zone, stable:"Pace looks good — hold steady" No HR signal:"Searching for HR signal"
Layer 2: Event Routing (When + What to Say)
Alert timing — you must be out of zone for 15 seconds before the first alert. After an alert, there's a 30-second cooldown before the next one. This prevents nagging.
Coaching events — the system recognizes 11 distinct situations:
🔴 SLOW_DOWN — above zone too long
🔵 SPEED_UP — below zone too long
✅ RETURN_TO_ZONE — back in zone
⚠️ PREDICTIVE_WARNING — in zone but drifting
🔀 SEGMENT_CHANGE — new interval started
❌ SIGNAL_LOST — HR monitor disconnected
📡 SIGNAL_REGAINED — HR reconnected
⏳ HALFWAY — 50% of workout done
📏 KM_SPLIT — passed another kilometer
🏁 WORKOUT_COMPLETE — target reached
😌 IN_ZONE_CONFIRM — every 3 min of silence
Predictive warnings — unique to Cardea: when you're currently IN zone but the prediction engine sees you drifting out, you get a gentle heads-up before you actually leave the zone. Fires at most once per 60 seconds. Only when confidence is MEDIUM or HIGH.
Layer 3: Audio Delivery (How Urgently)
Escalation ladder — the more you ignore alerts, the more insistent they become:
1st alert: Earcon only — a short musical tone. Subtle.
2nd alert: Earcon + Voice — tone followed by a spoken instruction ("Speed up" / "Slow down")
3rd+ alert: Earcon + Voice + Vibration — adds a haptic pulse to your phone
Resets to level 1 when you return to zone.
Voice verbosity — user chooses OFF (no voice ever), MINIMAL (only speed up/slow down/segment change/signal lost), or FULL (all events including return-to-zone, predictive warnings, km splits, halfway, and workout complete).
Music-friendly — audio plays over the navigation channel, layering on top of music without pausing it or stealing audio focus.

Post-Run Learning
The adaptive profile absorbs what happened this run — becoming a better model of your body
▼
Long-term trim update — if your average HR ran higher than your average target, the trim shifts upward so next time the system predicts your HR more accurately. Blends 92% old + 8% session error. After 10+ sessions, this captures your personal HR bias very precisely.
Response lag refinement — averages how long it took you to settle back into zone this session, and blends it with the stored lag estimate. 85% old + 15% observed. Captures changes in your cardiovascular fitness over time.
Pace-HR bucket merge — your running pace is divided into buckets (0.25 min/km wide). Each bucket stores the average HR you produce at that pace. Session data is merged with your lifetime profile, building a personal pace-HR curve.
HRmax auto-calibration — if 8+ consecutive seconds of HR exceed your current max (and it's not a cadence lock artifact), your HRmax is updated. This means all %HRmax workout targets become more accurate over time.
📊
Metrics Derived
Performance numbers computed from the raw run data
▼
Efficiency Factor — speed ÷ heart rate. Higher = more fit. Calculated as (meters/min) / HR. A rising EF over weeks means you're getting faster at the same heart rate.
Aerobic Decoupling — how much your EF drops in the second half vs. the first half. <5% = excellent aerobic base. >10% = your aerobic system fatigues quickly.
TRIMP — "training impulse" score combining duration and intensity. Feeds the CTL/ATL training load tracker.
HRR1 — heart rate recovery: how many bpm your HR drops in the first minute after stopping. A declining HRR1 over time can signal overtraining or illness.

Fitness Signal Evaluation
"Should your training get harder, easier, or stay the same?" — analyzes trends across your recent runs
▼
Training Stress Balance (TSB) — your "freshness" score. Compares your long-term fitness load (CTL) against your short-term fatigue (ATL).
TSB < -25 → you're overtrained → EASE BACK
TSB > +5 AND fitness is rising → you're fresh and adapting → PUSH HARDER
Otherwise → HOLD
Efficiency Factor trend — is your EF rising or falling over recent reliable sessions? Rising + fresh = green light to push. Falling may signal overreaching.
HRR1 trend — is your recovery speed declining? A drop of 10+ bpm in HRR1 across sessions triggers EASE_BACK regardless of other signals. This is a strong early warning of overtraining or illness.
Illness detection — the killer feature of this system. If you're rested (TSB positive) but both your EF and HRR1 are crashing simultaneously, the most likely explanation isn't overtraining — it's that you're getting sick. SOFT tier = HRR1 crash alone. FULL tier = HRR1 + EF crash together. Full illness flag overrides everything → EASE_BACK.
Output → PUSH_HARDER, HOLD, or EASE_BACK — this signal flows directly into the tier system and training plan.

The Tier System
Controls workout complexity — how hard and varied your training gets. Four tiers from "easy runs only" to "full periodized racing."
▼
How your tier is set — your goal sets a base tier: Cardio Health = 1, 5K/10K race = 2, Half Marathon = 3, Marathon = 4. A tier index (starting at 0) adjusts up or down from there. Effective tier = goal.tier + tierIndex - 1, clamped to 1-4.
What each tier unlocks:
Tier 1
All easy runs + long runs. Pure aerobic base-building. No quality sessions.
Tier 2
Adds tempo runs (Z3 pace) + strides during BUILD phase. Long runs get proportionally longer.
Tier 3
Adds threshold runs (Z4) + VO2max intervals (Norwegian 4x4). Long runs become race simulations in PEAK phase. Two quality sessions per week during PEAK.
Tier 4
Full periodization — VO2max intervals take priority, threshold as secondary quality session. Maximum workout variety and intensity.
Tier movement — driven by the Fitness Signal Evaluator's tuning direction. If your body is adapting well (PUSH_HARDER), the system can recommend moving you up a tier. If you're struggling (EASE_BACK), it can dial you back down.
Finishing time connection — your target finishing time (stored on enrollment) can also influence tier placement, anchoring difficulty to your race goals.

Training Plan Engine
Generates your weekly schedule — what to run, how long, and which preset workout to use
▼
Phase periodization — training cycles through 4 phases, each lasting several weeks:
BASE (3-6 wk): all easy running — builds aerobic foundation
BUILD (4-6 wk): introduces quality sessions — tempo, strides
PEAK (2-3 wk): highest intensity — intervals, race sims
TAPER (1-2 wk): reduced volume — sharpening for race day
Duration scaling — uses Daniels' Running Formula ratios to compute session lengths from your target minutes:
Long run: 1.25-1.5× easy run (bigger spike when fewer runs/week)
Tempo: 10% of weekly total, capped 15-40 min
Interval: 8% of weekly total, capped 12-35 min
Easy runs absorb the remainder to keep weekly total constant
Recovery weeks — every 3rd week automatically reduces volume to 80%. Prevents burnout accumulation.
Preset workouts — each planned session maps to a specific workout preset (Zone 2 Base, Aerobic Tempo, Lactate Threshold, Norwegian 4x4, etc.) which defines exact HR targets as %HRmax with segment structure.

How Systems Connect
🔗
Key Cross-System Connections
Adaptive Profile → Fitness Signals — the profile's CTL/ATL values (training load) are the primary input for determining whether to push or ease.
Fitness Signals → Tier System — the tuning direction (push/hold/ease) decides if your tier goes up, stays, or goes down.
Tier → Training Plan — your effective tier determines which workout types appear in your weekly schedule.
Training Plan → Zone Engine — the plan's workout config (target HR, segments, buffer) defines the zone boundaries the engine evaluates against.
Zone Engine → Adaptive Prediction → Coaching Language — zone status feeds into prediction, prediction feeds into guidance text and warning events.
Quality Filters → Learning — sessions flagged as environment-affected or artifact-contaminated are excluded from fitness trend analysis, protecting the model from bad data.
⏱️
Timescales
Milliseconds — HR sampling, zone evaluation, guidance text updates. The tightest feedback loop.
Seconds — short-term trim reacts within a single run. HR slope tracks moment-to-moment trends. Alert cooldowns operate on 15-30 second timers.
Sessions — long-term trim, response lag, and pace-HR buckets update after each run. Gets noticeably better after 4+ sessions.
Weeks — fitness signals (CTL/ATL/TSB, EF trend, HRR1 trend) evaluate over the last 42 days. Tuning direction changes weekly.
Months — phase periodization (Base → Build → Peak → Taper) unfolds over 10-17 weeks. Tier adjustments happen at week boundaries.
